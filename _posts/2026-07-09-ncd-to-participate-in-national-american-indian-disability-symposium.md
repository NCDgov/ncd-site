**For Immediate Release**

July 9, 2026

**WASHINGTON--**The National Council on Disability (NCD) will take part in the 2026 National American Indian Disability Symposium July 16-17. 

The free, two-day virtual gathering via Zoom runs 8 a.m. – 4 p.m. MST (Arizona Time) under the theme "Cultivating Care: The Three Sisters Approach to Disability in Native Country," and focuses on community, kinship, and inclusion. 

At noon on July 16, NCD representatives provide a brief history of NCD reports on American Indians and Alaska Natives, providing a brief synopsis of our [2003](https://www.ncd.gov/report/people-with-disabilities-on-tribal-lands-education-health-care-vocational-rehabilitation-and-independent-living/) report, [2003](https://www.ncd.gov/report/understanding-disabilities-in-american-indian-and-alaska-native-communities-toolkit-guide/) and [2023](https://www.ncd.gov/report/understanding-disabilities-in-american-indian-alaska-native-communities-toolkit-guide/) toolkits, and talk about the most recent research for the updated report to be released in November.

The event is hosted by the Native American Disability Law Center, Inter-Tribal Disability Advocacy Council, and University of Arizona Sonoran Center for Excellence in Disabilities.

Register in advance at: https://arizona.zoom.us/meeting/register/0ClYDsyXTPSIeY15qSPfbA#/registration

Agenda and speaker biographies are available [here.](https://sonorancenter.arizona.edu/sites/default/files/2026-07/Agenda-and-Speaker-Bios.pdf)

ASL and CART will be provided.

NCD is an independent federal agency making disability policy recommendations to the President, Congress, federal agencies, State, tribal communities, and local governments.

Please contact event organizers via email at kimberly.yellowrobe@bannerhealth.com or agnesa@arizona.edu for for questions on the symposium.
